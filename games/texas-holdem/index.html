    How Hold’em, betting, and the three tables work

    Make the best five

    Combine your two private cards with five shared community cards. Your final hand is the strongest five-card combination available from those seven; you do not have to use both private cards.

    Four betting rounds

    • Pre-flop: after the small and big blinds.
    • Flop: three shared cards.
    • Turn: a fourth shared card.
    • River: the fifth shared card, followed by showdown.

    Choices & all-ins

    Check when no bet is owed, call to match, raise by at least the previous full raise, or fold. A short stack may call or raise all-in for less. Heads-up wagers are capped by the chips the opponent can match, so there is no side pot.

    Hand order

    Straight flush, four of a kind, full house, flush, straight, three of a kind, two pair, one pair, then high card. Kickers break ties; an ace can be high or low in A-2-3-4-5.

    Three real opponents

    Rookie uses loose rules and random sizing. Regular reads made hands, draws, pressure, and pot odds. Shark estimates equity by simulating unseen cards and mixes value bets with occasional bluffs.

    Heads-up details

    The dealer posts the small blind and acts first before the flop. The big blind acts first after the flop. The dealer button alternates every hand. Press C to check/call, F to fold, or R to raise.
