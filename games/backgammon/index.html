Rules, scoring & controls

Move & enter

  • Move one checker per die. Doubles play four times. Use both dice when possible; if only one can be used, play the higher one.
  • A point with two or more opposing checkers is blocked. Land on a lone blot to hit it to the bar.
  • Checkers on the bar must re-enter in the opponent's home board before any other checker may move.

Bear off & win

  • Once all 15 checkers are in your home board, exact rolls bear off. An oversize roll may bear off your farthest checker.
  • A normal win is 1 point. If the loser bears off none, it is a gammon (2). If they are also on the bar or in the winner's home board, it is a backgammon (3).

Controls & AI

Click/tap a movable checker, then a gold destination. The bar and home tray are selectable. R rolls, U undoes a move during your turn, Esc clears selection, and N starts a new game. Easy is random; Medium is tactical; Hard compares bounded opponent replies across representative rolls.
